What is it

What is an aerobatic flight?

An aerobatic flight is a sport flight in a certified aerobatic aircraft during which the pilot performs manoeuvres unavailable in passenger aviation: loops, rolls, inverted flight, hammerheads, and knife-edge passes. Unlike a scenic flight or introductory lesson, full aerobatics involves the complete G-force envelope up to +6G. At akrobacja.com flights depart in an Extra 300L SP-EKS from Radom-Piastow airport (EPRP), 100 km / 60 miles from Warsaw. Every flight is piloted by Maciej Kulaszewski, 2022 World Aerobatic Champion, FCL.800-certified FI(A) instructor with over 3,500 hours on type.

Travelling from abroad? Radom-Piastow (EPRP) is 90 minutes by car from Warsaw Chopin (WAW) via the S7 motorway. Free parking on site. We can recommend hotels in Radom for overnight guests.

The aircraft

Extra 300L SP-EKS

Extra 300L SP-EKS

The Extra 300L is one of the world's finest aerobatic aircraft, used by top competition pilots at international championships. Our example SP-EKS is the two-seat tandem variant, built specifically for flying with a passenger.

The airframe is certified for -10G to +10G. Passenger flights operate at up to +6G: roughly 30% of the structural limit, with a safety margin of more than three times. The aircraft is powered by a Lycoming AEIO-580 flat-six, producing 315 hp on Avgas. Polish craftsmanship: maintained to EASA Part-M standards in Poland.
