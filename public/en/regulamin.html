I. General provisions

  1. These Terms and Conditions set out the rules for the sale of vouchers for introductory flights on the aerobatic aircraft Extra 300L (registration mark SP-EKS) through the akrobacja.com website (hereinafter: the "Website").
  2. The Website owner and voucher seller is Doradztwo Paweł Mamcarz, registered at ul. Jesionowa 18, 05-825 Grodzisk Mazowiecki, Poland, Tax ID (NIP): 7122188089 (hereinafter: the "Operator").
  3. Introductory flights are performed in accordance with:
    1. Commission Regulation (EU) No 965/2012 of 5 October 2012 (Article 6(4a)(c)),
    2. Guidelines No. 9/2025 of the President of the Civil Aviation Authority (ULC) of 9 July 2025 on the conduct of introductory flights,
    3. Visual Flight Rules (VFR) by day,
    4. NCO (Non-Commercial Operations) requirements specified in Annex VII to Regulation 965/2012.
  4. The Operator is a declared training organisation authorised to conduct introductory flights in accordance with § 3 of Guidelines No. 9/2025 of the President of the Civil Aviation Authority.
  5. The aircraft Extra 300L (SP-EKS) is a non-complex aeroplane holding a valid Certificate of Airworthiness (CofA) issued in accordance with Commission Regulation (EU) No 748/2012 (§ 4(1) of the Guidelines).

II. Definitions

  1. Voucher - a document (in PDF form) confirming the acquisition of the right to take an introductory flight in the selected package, containing a unique alphanumeric code.
  2. Introductory flight - an aerial operation within the meaning of Article 2(9) of Regulation 965/2012, i.e. a flight against remuneration or other form of consideration consisting of a short round flight intended to introduce someone to aviation or to attract new pupils or members of an organisation.
  3. Customer - a natural person who purchases a Voucher through the Website.
  4. Participant - a person taking the introductory flight on the basis of the Voucher (the Customer or a person designated by the Customer).
  5. Pilot-in-command - a person holding the qualifications set out in § 5 of Guidelines No. 9/2025 of the President of the Civil Aviation Authority, personally performing the flight.

III. Packages and prices

  1. The Operator offers the following packages:
    1. First Flight - introductory flight: ground briefing and preparation (approx. 20 min) + flight 10-12 minutes, including basic aerobatic figures. No prior aviation experience required. Price: 1,999 PLN;
    2. Adrenaline - introductory flight: briefing (approx. 20 min) + flight of approx. 20 minutes with a full aerobatic programme + debriefing after the flight. No prior aviation experience required. Price: 2,999 PLN;
    3. Para - package of 2 First Flight rides for a couple: 2× (briefing 20 min + flight 10-12 minutes) in one session, back-to-back. Each partner flies solo (the Extra 300L seats the pilot plus one passenger). Price: 3,777 PLN;
    4. Para Adrenaline - package of 2 Adrenaline rides for a couple: 2× (briefing 20 min + flight approx. 20 minutes full aerobatic programme + debrief) in one session, back-to-back. Each partner flies solo. Price: 5,999 PLN;
    5. Masterclass - training session for pilots holding a PPL(A) licence: briefing + 2 flights (up to 50 minutes total airborne time) + debriefing covering each figure. Includes spin recovery and advanced figures. A valid PPL(A) licence is required. Price: 4,999 PLN.
  2. Each package may be supplemented with the 360° Flight Video add-on (post-production + MP4 file) for an additional fee of 299 PLN.
  3. All prices are gross prices (VAT included).
  4. The maximum planned duration of a single flight does not exceed 50 minutes, in accordance with § 6(4) of the Guidelines.
  5. The Masterclass package requires the Participant to hold a valid PPL(A) licence or higher. The Operator reserves the right to verify the licence before the flight.

IV. Voucher purchase procedure

  1. A voucher is purchased through the Website by:
    1. selecting a package and optional add-ons,
    2. completing the order form (full name, email address, optionally VAT ID for an invoice),
    3. completing payment through the Stripe payment processor (payment card, Przelewy24, BLIK).
  2. Once payment is confirmed, the Customer receives:
    1. the voucher as a PDF file sent to the email address provided,
    2. the option to download the voucher from the Website using a unique code,
    3. a VAT invoice or receipt (at the Customer's request).
  3. The sales contract is concluded at the moment the payment is confirmed by the payment processor.

V. Voucher validity and scheduling

  1. The voucher is valid for 12 months from the date of purchase.
  2. The flight date is agreed individually with the Operator by phone (+48 535 535 221) or email (info@akrobacja.com).
  3. The flight begins and ends at Radom-Piastów airfield (EPRP), entered in the register of airfields, in accordance with § 6(3) of the Guidelines.
  4. The Operator reserves the right to refuse to perform the flight or to change the date due to:
    1. adverse meteorological conditions that prevent VFR flight,
    2. technical limitations of the aircraft,
    3. airfield operating limitations,
    4. decisions of Air Traffic Services (ATS).
  5. Should a flight be cancelled for the reasons indicated in point 4, the Customer is entitled to rebook a new date free of charge. The voucher validity period is extended by the length of any delay caused by the Operator.
  6. After the validity period expires, the voucher loses its validity. An unused voucher is not refundable unless required by law.

VI. Participant requirements

  1. The minimum age of the Participant is 13 years (§ 7 of the Guidelines). Minors may take part only with the written consent of their legal guardians.
  2. On board the aircraft, in addition to the pilot-in-command, there may be no more than 1 person (the Extra 300L is a two-seat aircraft).
  3. The Participant's maximum body weight is approximately 110 kg and maximum height approximately 195 cm, as imposed by the cockpit dimensions of the Extra 300L.
  4. The Participant should be in generally good health. Contraindications include in particular:
    1. serious cardiac conditions,
    2. epilepsy,
    3. pregnancy,
    4. recent surgical procedures,
    5. being under the influence of alcohol or psychoactive substances.
  5. The Participant is obliged to inform the Operator of any circumstances that may affect the safety of the flight.
  6. The Operator reserves the right to refuse to perform the flight in case of justified doubts as to the health condition or sobriety of the Participant.

VII. Conduct of the introductory flight

  1. Before the flight, the Participant receives a briefing on the course of the flight, safety rules, use of on-board equipment and behaviour in emergency situations.
  2. The flight is personally conducted by a pilot-in-command holding the qualifications set out in § 5 of Guidelines No. 9/2025 of the President of the Civil Aviation Authority, in particular:
    1. a valid pilot licence with instructor privileges,
    2. documented experience of at least three take-offs and three landings on the Extra 300L in the last 90 days,
    3. knowledge of the area where flights are performed.
  3. The flight is conducted according to VFR rules by day (§ 4 of the Guidelines).
  4. The Operator ensures two-way communication with Air Traffic Services (ATS) during the flight (§ 6(2) of the Guidelines).
  5. For each flight, safe landing locations are designated from every point of the route in case of a powerplant failure (§ 6(5) of the Guidelines).
  6. The pilot-in-command may at any time decide to interrupt or modify the flight programme for safety reasons. The decision of the pilot-in-command is final and cannot be appealed against.
  7. The Participant is obliged to comply unconditionally with the instructions of the pilot-in-command and ground staff.

VIII. Insurance and liability

  1. The Extra 300L aircraft (SP-EKS) carries mandatory third-party and passenger liability insurance, in accordance with Regulation (EC) No 785/2004.
  2. The Participant is covered by accident insurance (NNW) for the duration of the flight. Policy details are available on request.
  3. The Operator shall not be liable for damages resulting from:
    1. the Participant concealing information about their health condition,
    2. failure to follow the instructions of the pilot-in-command or ground staff,
    3. acts of force majeure.
